1. CaseFlow is an agentic alert-resolution workspace for GoTyme's anti-money-laundering and
know-your-transaction analysts. It begins where monitoring ends: at an alert. All data shown is
synthetic. This case has an incomplete Travel Rule payload, and the analyst's goal is explicit:
reach a defensible next action, not blindly trust the vendor score.

2. When the analyst runs CaseFlow, a live language-model agent plans, then calls tools. It fetches
the customer profile, the transaction, wallet intelligence, and the Travel Rule payload, and it
searches the versioned policy corpus. Each tool call carries real arguments, and every step becomes
a timestamped, SHA-256-hashed audit event. Nothing is fabricated.

3. A second language-model role, the Compliance Challenger, adversarially reviews the decision.
Here, beneficiary identity and beneficiary VASP are missing. Under clause TR-1.2 the evidence is
incomplete, so CaseFlow returns REQUEST_EVIDENCE and names exactly what is missing. A deterministic
guardrail guarantees an incomplete Travel Rule payload can never be sealed as clear or escalate.

4. The analyst adds the seeded beneficiary payload and resumes the same case. No audit trail is
lost. The agent re-investigates with the now-complete evidence. A residency contradiction remains,
so under clause KYC-2.4 it now recommends ESCALATE, with calibrated confidence, cited clauses,
evidence identifiers, and an analyst-ready narrative. It is still a recommendation: the analyst
accepts, overrides with a reason, or requests more evidence.

5. Nothing here is asserted. Running the real agent over twenty labelled synthetic cases, CaseFlow
measured ninety percent disposition agreement, one hundred percent policy-citation recall, and one
hundred percent request-evidence recall, with zero fabricated packets. The labels live outside the
runtime prompts, and misses are reported honestly.

6. This is CaseFlow's core idea: non-deterministic reasoning inside deterministic governance. The
model plans, investigates, and argues, while typed contracts, round limits, and hard evidence gates
decide what can be sealed. And learning stays human-gated: analyst feedback drafts a versioned
policy proposal, never a silent change.
